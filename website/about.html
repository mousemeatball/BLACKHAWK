Who we are

About AEC

A student-run aerospace club at Mira Costa High School, building rockets and running simulations since our founding.

Our story

Built from the ground up.

The Mira Costa Aerospace Club was founded by students who wanted to do more than read about aerospace — they wanted to build it. What started as a small group has grown into a 60+ member organization with two active divisions and a national competition presence.

Today AEC has two divisions: Rocketry and Simulation & CAD. Every project is entirely student-led, from the initial CAD model and SimScale CFD analysis through to launch day at Lucerne Dry Lake.

We compete in TARC (Team America Rocketry Challenge) and NRC (National Rocketry Competition) — two of the most competitive high school rocketry events in the country.

Our rocketry division operates in partnership with the Hermosa Beach Rocketry Club, giving students access to experienced mentors, certified range safety officers, and real high-power launch days.

What drives us

Our values

The principles behind every build, simulation, and flight.

01

Build real things

No passive learning. Every member builds something real that flies — or spectacularly doesn't.

02

Engineer first

We simulate before we build. SimScale CFD and CAD come before the airframe, every time.

03

Compete seriously

TARC and NRC demand precision. We hold ourselves to competition standards on every project.

04

Teach each other

Senior members mentor new ones. Every person who learns becomes someone who teaches.
